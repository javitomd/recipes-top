Arepa

Arepas are a traditional venezuelan dish, prepared with very few ingredients, but very delicious. This is what venezuelan families usually eat for breakfast and dinner.

There are no limits to what you can put inside of them, which also makes them very flexible and versatile when it comes to flavor.

However, for this recipe we'll keep it simple, as the most traditional way to eat them is with just butter and cheese. Easy, fast and yummy! Let's get to it...

For this recipe we'll need the following ingredients

Ingredients:

  • Bowl
  • Pan
  • Corn flour
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Butter
  • Mozzarella

Preparation:

  1. Pour the water into the bowl.
  2. Add some salt to the water and mix it.
  3. Put the pan on the stove and turn it on low.
  4. Gradually add the corn flower to the salted water.
  5. Mix all the ingredients very well until you get an uniform dough.
  6. Take a small part of the dough to make a ball with it.
  7. Press the ball with your hands until the ball looks like a circle.
    • Once your circle looks nice, put it in the preheated pan.
  8. Repeat steps 6 and 7 until you run out of dough.
  9. Cook the arepas until they get a golden color and tap them with a knife to check they are crunchy.

Once your arepas look similar to the ones on the picture, get them out of the pan and put them on a plate.

  1. Grate your mozarella cheese and have it ready.
  2. Open your arepas with a knife.
  3. Spread some butter inside it and put your cheese in.
  4. Let the arepas rest for 2 minutes while the cheese melts.

And voila! You can go ahead and enjoy your delicious arepas
